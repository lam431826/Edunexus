Detailed Notes

The lecture begins by emphasizing the fundamental differences between classical mechanics, where properties are definite, and quantum mechanics, which relies on probability and uncertainty (referencing Heisenberg). The primary example discussed is the double-slit experiment, illustrating how individual electrons display interference patterns, demonstrating wave-like behavior even when fired as discrete particles.
